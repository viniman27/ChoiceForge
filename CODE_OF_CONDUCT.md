# Contributor Code of Conduct

ChoiceForge is a small open-source project; we want everyone who interacts with it — through issues, pull requests, discussions, or any other channel tied to this repository — to have a good experience.

## In short

- **Be respectful.** Disagreement is fine; condescension, harassment, personal attacks, and discriminatory language are not.
- **Assume good faith.** Most contributors are juggling this between other priorities and writing in a non-native language. Read charitably and ask for clarification before pushing back.
- **Stay focused on the work.** Critique code and ideas, not people.
- **Respect the maintainers' time.** We respond when we can. Bumping a thread once after a week is fine; repeated nagging is not.
- **No spam, no off-topic promotion, no surveillance-flavoured behaviour** (data scraping of contributors, automated harassment, etc.).

## Unacceptable behaviour

The following will get you a warning, then a block, depending on severity:

- Personal insults, slurs, or harassment based on race, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, religion, nationality, age, body type, neurotype, or any other personal characteristic.
- Sexualized language or imagery anywhere in the project.
- Public or private threats of any kind.
- Deliberately misgendering or deadnaming someone.
- Sustained disruption of issues, PRs, or discussions.
- Publishing other people's private information ("doxxing").

## Enforcement

Reports about violations go to the maintainer privately:

- **viniciusdearaujo27@gmail.com** (subject line: `[ChoiceForge CoC]`)

Include the GitHub URLs of the relevant comments, a short description of what happened, and how you'd like it handled. Reports will be read within 7 days. If we agree there's a violation we'll act proportionally — anything from a private clarification to a permanent block from the repository.

We won't disclose the identity of reporters to the subject of the report without consent.

## Scope

This applies to:

- The `viniman27/ChoiceForge` GitHub repository, including issues, PRs, and discussions.
- Any release announcements, social posts, or chat rooms officially associated with the project.

It does not cover unrelated interactions between contributors elsewhere on the internet, though severe off-platform behaviour may still affect our willingness to keep working with someone here.
